NUSH Exam Guide

Written for a Year 1 audience

This guide is meant as a simplified explanation of NUS High's examination rules and regulations (found in Section 5 of the student handbook). If there are any discrepancies between this page and the official handbook, please always refer to the handbook!

Attendance & punctuality

Illness

If you are sick, do not report to school for the exam. Get a MC on the same day and submit it to the Admin Office within 48 hours of the exam (use the email address above).

What if I have COVID?

If you have COVID, you do not need to get a MC. Instead, do an ART self-test, take a picture of the positive test with your name and the date written on the kit and send it to the Admin Office.

Train disruption

If you are caught in a train disruption, inform the Admin Office immediately and continue making your way to school. Make sure you tell the Admin Office what time you expect to arrive (or at least where you are) too.

You may also find this guide by SGTrains on what to do in a train disruption helpful.

Flooding-related traffic jams

If you are stuck in traffic due to a flood, inform the Admin Office immediately and continue making your way to school. Make sure you tell the Admin Office where you are too.

Note that for exams, an MC is the only valid reason for absence. If you are late for the exam for any reason besides a train disruption or flooding, you will not be given extra time to complete the paper.

Attire & belongings

Attire

You must wear proper school attire for exams. Failure to do so can result in getting zero marks for the exam.

Note that if you wear a sweater/jacket with a hood to the exam, you are not allowed to put the hood over your head while in the exam venue.

Belongings

Place your bag(s), phones and other belongings in your locker (at your classroom) before the exam starts. (You may wish to empty out your locker before the exam period to make this easier.)

You MUST also bring your NUS High Student ID card (not your EZ-Link card) for the exam - if you didn't, go to the Admin Office for assistance.

Stationery

You cannot bring a pencil case inside the exam venue. Instead, use a transparent ziploc bag to store your stationery.

Allowed stationery
  • Non-erasable black or dark blue pens
  • 2B pencils, erasers and sharpeners
  • Rulers
  • Highlighters
  • For relevant exams only:
    • Approved calculators
    • Approved e-dictionary or hardcopy dictionary
    • Mathematical set (without formula sheet)
    You can also bring spare approved devices and/or batteries. Note that "approved" here means having the official school approval sticker. Do not bring your calculator or e-dictionary's cover into the exam venue.

Exam protocols

Reporting

Report to your exam venue (not your classroom!) at least 30 minutes before the start of the exam (so if your exam starts at 8 a.m, be at the venue by 7.30).

Make sure you check your seat number before entering the venue (you're not allowed to communicate once inside the venue). Make sure also to bring your Student ID card.

During the exam

No eating or drinking is allowed during the exam, except for plain water. If you feel unwell during the exam, alert an invigilator immediately.

Please only use 2B pencils on your OMS. Do not use correction tape or correction fluid on any part of your paper.

End of the exam

Stop writing (and/or shading your OMS) when you are told to do so. Do not communicate until you have left the exam room.

Whatever you didn't bring into the exam room must stay in the exam room.
